  • For many, hiking in Peru is synonymous with hiking to Machu Picchu. The former Inca city is one of them most famous sights, not only in Peru but throughout South America. And no wonder, this one historical site was named one of the world's seven wonders in 2007

  • Teotihuacán (pronounced " tay-oh-tee-wah-KAHN" with the emphasis on the final syllable) is a large and majestic archaeological site located about 25 miles (40 km) north of Mexico City. It is famous for its large pyramids dedicated to the sun and the moon, but the site also contains beautiful murals and carvings and several museums through which you can explore the city's fascinating history. This is one of the largest and most important archaeological sites in Mexico

  • Tanzania offers world-class visitor safaris with Serengeti National Park and Ngorongoro Volcano Crater as highlights. If you are lucky and arrive at the right time of year, you can also experience the great migration of wildebeest and zebras, as hundreds of thousands of animals move between Kenya and Tanzania in search of food. Equally exciting are the climbs to Africa's highest mountain, Kilimanjaro.
